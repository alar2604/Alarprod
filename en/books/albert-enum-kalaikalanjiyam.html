About the Editor

S. Arputharaj was acquainted with Professor Albert for seventy years, since his youth. He retired as the Assistant Headmaster from St. Joseph's Higher Secondary School, Trichy. He has written some short stories and poems and possesses a keen appreciation for literature.

Life-Changing Perspective

Priya Sharma | March 15, 2023

This book came at exactly the right time in my life. I was feeling overwhelmed with too many priorities and unable to make progress on any of them. The practical techniques for identifying what truly matters and creating systems to focus on those areas have been transformative. I especially appreciated the chapter on digital minimalism.

Practical and Insightful

Rajiv Patel | February 28, 2023

As a business owner, I'm constantly juggling multiple priorities. This book provided me with clear strategies to identify my most important work and create time blocks to focus on those activities. The author's personal examples made the concepts relatable, and the worksheets at the end of each chapter helped me apply the ideas immediately. Highly recommended for anyone feeling stretched thin.

Good But Not Revolutionary

Arjun Krishnan | February 10, 2023

While this book has some useful advice, much of it feels like a rehash of other productivity books I've read. The writing style is accessible and the exercises are helpful, but I was hoping for more innovative approaches. That said, if you're new to productivity literature, this would be a solid introduction with practical applications.
